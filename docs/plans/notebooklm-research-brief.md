# SabaiApply — Research Brief for NotebookLM

## Company in 3 lines

**SabaiApply** is a Thai university admissions SaaS (Next.js + Supabase, live at sabaiapply.com). We're pivoting to **university-first B2B** — universities pay, students use it free. MVP scope: **TCAS Round 1 (Portfolio)**. 2 co-founders in the KMITL/CMKL Zero-to-1 accelerator. Interview 2026-04-24. Goal: 300k THB MRR.

## Grounding (don't re-research)

- **Market:** 85 Thai universities, ~4,876 programs on TCAS. mytcas.com has a public program catalog but no deadline data — those live in per-uni PDFs.
- **Status quo stack:** Excel + email + LINE + in-house PHP portals. Faculties manually re-type applications.
- **Admissions officer pain (Round 1):** eligibility screening from messy PDFs, chasing missing docs, interview scheduling, TCAS 48-hour confirmation window, CUPT reporting.
- **ICP shortlist:** KMITL OIA, Assumption, Bangkok U, Rangsit, Sripatum, Dhurakij Pundit.
- **Bootstrap plan:** first 3–5 universities at symbolic price (or free) in exchange for being listed + testimonial + workflow access. Students won't come until real unis are listed.

## What I need from you

### 1. Market & competitors

- Thai unis by segment (public/private, tier) — which segment is most likely to pay and why.
- Who sells admissions software in Thailand today? (Slate, Ellucian, Salesforce Edu, local vendors, in-house.) Pricing, feature gaps, contract structure.
- TCAS-adjacent tools (dek-d, admissionpremium, TCASFolio, iFolio) — what do they do well/poorly?

### 2. Pain points

- Rank top 10 pain points for a Thai Round 1 admissions officer. Mark each "annoying" vs "painful enough to pay." Flag which are unsolved by existing tools and which hit **private universities hardest**.

### 3. Pricing — this is the priority

Find **real Thai-specific numbers**, not US extrapolation. Sources to check:

- Thai gov't procurement: e-GP, gprocurement.go.th (RFPs/contracts for admissions, CRM, SIS, ERP at universities).
- Public Thai uni IT budgets (Chula, Mahidol, KMITL, Thammasat, Kasetsart).
- Private uni annual reports / digital-transformation press (Rangsit, Bangkok U, Sripatum).
- Indirect signals: Thai uni job postings mentioning Slate/Salesforce/Ellucian, vendor case studies naming Thai customers.
- SEA comparables (Malaysia/Singapore/Indonesia/Vietnam) only if no Thai data.

Report a **table of THB figures** with source URL + date + confidence (high/med/low). **If no Thai source exists, say "no evidence found" — do not infer from US prices.**

Also tell me:

- Typical first-year contract size for higher-ed SaaS in Thailand.
- Preferred billing model (per-application / per-seat / per-faculty / flat annual).
- Who signs: Registrar, OIA, IT, or President's office.

### 4. Pilot strategy

- B2B SaaS case studies: symbolic-priced lighthouse pilots → what did the **next cohort** pay? What's the typical multiple?
- Standard contract terms for "design partner" pilots (testimonial clause, renewal lock-in, most-favored-pricing protection).
- How many pilot wins before a new uni pays full price with no discount?
- What to say when a prospect asks "why should we pay if the pilots didn't?"

### 5. Accelerator pitch output

- Strongest 1-sentence positioning for a Thai university buyer.
- Top 3 CIO/Registrar objections + strongest counters.
- **Proposed 3-tier pricing ladder in THB** (pilot / early adopter / standard) with rationale.
- **Path to 300k THB MRR:** how many unis at each tier?

## Output format

For each section: **Key findings** (cited) → **Action items** (1–3) → **Confidence** (H/M/L).

Finish with a **one-page executive summary** I can paste straight into a Gamma slide, and a **red-flag list** of any pricing assumption the evidence contradicts.
